About AMOS



Background

Construction of AMOS began in March 2006 and continues to this day.
The AMOS dataset contains images focusing on natural environments taken from webcams located around the world, the vast majority in the United States.
This database is unique because it contains many natural outdoor scenes in consecutive and continuous periods.

                   


Using The Data

Collecting and storing all of the data is useful for studying changes in

climate
,
environment
,
temperature
, and
behavior
of objects in the view of these webcams. The idea is to look at changes in the environment and analyze what could have brought up those changes. For example, if a bike lane is implemented, we want to be able to quantify
how many
people use the bike lane versus the sidewalk.
The ideas for analysis of this data are limitless and continuing to this day.
This data set has been used for studies in
webcam geolocation
,
geo-orientation
, and
scene annotation
.

Images

The AMOS dataset consists of over

40 million
images captured since March 2006 from
1,000
outdoor webcams located primarily in the continental United States.
This dataset is unique in that it contains significantly more scenes than in previous datasets of natural images from static cameras. Images from each camera are captured several times per hour using a custom web crawler that ignores duplicate images and records the capture time.

                   


Webcams

AMOS is home to

36,188
publicly available webcams.
21,015
of these cameras have known geographic locations.
Once a camera has been added to AMOS, we begin taking snapshots of the camera feed every
30
minutes.
