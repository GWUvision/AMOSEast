Background

Construction of AMOS began in March 2006 and continues to this day. It is currently maintained
at The George Washington University by Robert Pless. The AMOS dataset contains billions of images focusing on different natural
environments taken from webcams located around the world. This database is unique because it contains many natural outdoor scenes over long time periods.



              


Using The Data

Collecting and storing all the data is useful for studying changes in environment, climate, temperature, and behavior of objects in the view of these webcams. The idea is to look at changes in the environment and analyze what could have caused those changes. For example, consider a bike lane introduced
to an area. The images of that area would show how many more bikes are being used as a result of this modification. The AMOS
dataset has been used for studies in webcam geolocation, geo-orientation, and scene annotation.

Images

The AMOS dataset consists of over 1 billion images captured since March 2006 from thousands of outdoor webcams mostly in the continental United States. This dataset is unique as it contains significantly more scenes than prior datasets of natural images from static cameras. Images from each camera
are captured multiple times per hour using a custom web crawler that ignores duplicate images and records the capture time.
